Geometry Chaos Editor Help

Timeline

Timeline is the place that helps you control objects' appearance and disappearance. Also, it visualises the audio track (if your map has one), which is helpful to sync objects with music.

Timeline controls

Above the timeline you can see some controls.

Play button plays the level as if it is playing in the game. Level will play until the end of the last object (music however won't stop). Pause button will pause the level.

Stop button stops the level playback and sets cursor position to the very start.

Object button creates a new object. On the timeline object will be placed at the cursor position on the first free track (more on cursor and tracks later).

After buttons comes cursor position spinner. It shows current cursor position and lets you jump to exact moments of the level with precision down to a millisecond.

Timeline

At the top of timeline you can see a bar of time ticks. You can drag mouse over the bar to control time cursor. Scrubbing over the level like that will update game field to show how your level will look at the exact moment in time.

The timeline consists of tracks. They are needed to better organize your objects. Timeline shows only 10 of them at once, but you can scroll down the timeline to reveal more. Number that appears on the left when you hover over the track is the index of this track.

To see objects that are located farther than you can currently see you can Shift+Scroll to scroll horizontally. Also, you can change the scale of the timeline using Ctrl+Scroll.

Controlling objects

On the timeline objects are displayed as the rectangles with the names. You can click on an object to select it. Selected object is highlighted on the timeline and on the game field. Information about the selected object is displayed in the object editor.

You can drag objects over the timeline to change time of their appearance and track that they are sitting on. Dragging from the right side of the object will allow you to set how long the object should be on screen.

Usually dragging actions are imprecise. To fix that, objects will try to stick to other objects or to the cursor. For example, when dragging an object, its start and end positions will try to stick to the start and end positions of other objects or cursor, if any of them are nearby. You can temporarily turn off this behaviour with holding down Alt while dragging.

Hotkeys

  • Delete: deletes selected object.

  • Shift+Delete: deletes all objects with the same name as of the selected object, except the selected object. In other words, deletes all parts of an array, except the selected one.

  • Ctrl+D: duplicates selected object.

  • Ctrl+Shift+D: opens Array creation dialog.

  • Ctrl+Q: trims each object on the track of the selected object so that they are ending at the cursor position (doesn't affect objects that are not under the cursor).

  • H: hides all unselected objects while pressed.
